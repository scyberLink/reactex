Crypto arbitrage trading is when people buy and sell cryptocurrencies on different exchanges to profit from price differences. They take advantage of the variations in prices to make money.

Let's say you see that Bitcoin (BTC) is being sold for $10,000 on Exchange A, but on Exchange B, it's being sold for $10,100.

  • You buy Bitcoin for $10,000 on Exchange A.
  • You then sell that same Bitcoin on Exchange B for $10,100.

    • After all those, you've made a $100 profit ($10,100 - $10,000) just by exploiting the price difference between the two exchanges.

    Normal crypto trading involves buying crypto asset with the intention of selling for profit when the price of the asset appreciates. It's typically done within a single market or exchange and can take hours, days, months or even years.

    Crypto arbitrage trading, on the other hand, involves exploiting price differences of a cryptocurrency across different exchanges. Traders aim to buy low on one exchange and sell high on another almost simultaneously, profiting from the variation in prices. These can happen in minutes even seconds.

    The key distinction lies in the strategy: normal trading seeks to profit from price movements over time, while crypto arbitrage trading capitalizes on price disparities across exchanges in real-time.
